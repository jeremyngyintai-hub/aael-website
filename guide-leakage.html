樓上漏水：責任誰屬、如何舉證與解決流程Water Seepage from Above: Responsibility, Evidence and Resolution

躍昇建築事務顧問有限公司　·　更新於 2026年7月Ascend Architecture & Engineering Ltd　·　Updated July 2026

漏水糾紛難處理，通常不是因為技術複雜，而是因為缺乏證據。天花有水漬人人看得見，但水從哪裡來、責任誰屬，若無專業勘察報告，往往各執一詞而多年不決。Seepage disputes drag on not because the technical problem is hard, but because the evidence is missing. Everyone can see the stain; without a professional investigation, nobody can agree where the water comes from.

常見的滲漏源頭

天花滲水未必來自樓上的地台防水層失效。實務上常見的來源包括：

  • 樓上單位的防水層老化或破損（尤其廁所、廚房及露台）
  • 供水管或排水管滲漏（可能在樓板內或牆內）
  • 公用喉管滲漏——責任屬於法團而非個別業主
  • 外牆滲水或窗框接縫失效，沿結構滲入
  • 冷氣機排水管或冷凝水積聚
  • 樓板本身出現裂縫，配合上層積水

來源不同，責任方就不同——這正是勘察的意義。

專業勘察怎樣做

合資格的勘察並非只憑目視。常用方法包括：

  • 濕度測試——量度不同位置的含水量，界定滲水範圍及方向
  • 紅外線熱成像——透過溫差找出結構內部的濕區分佈
  • 色水測試——在懷疑源頭注入顏色劑，觀察是否於滲漏位置出現
  • 盛水／封閉測試——分階段封閉或注水，逐一排除可能來源
  • 喉管檢查——必要時以內窺鏡檢查管道狀況
為何自行「拆開睇下」通常不划算

未經測試就開牆拆磚，即使找到源頭，亦已造成不必要的破壞；若找錯位置，更要重複多次。有系統的測試次序，目的正是以最少破壞找出最可能的來源。

報告的用途

一份完整的勘察報告，除了指出源頭及建議維修方法，更重要的是提供可用於溝通及追討的客觀依據。實務上它可以用於：

  • 與樓上業主或法團協商維修責任
  • 向政府部門（如聯合辦事處）提供技術資料
  • 保險索償
  • 民事索償或小額錢債審裁處程序

官方途徑與私人途徑

政府設有滲水投訴調查聯合辦事處，處理涉及衞生妨擾的滲水個案。此途徑免費，但有其局限：調查有既定次序及輪候時間，且並非所有滲漏個案都構成法例下的妨擾。

私人委聘專業勘察的優點是主動、快速、範圍可自行界定，並可即時取得完整報告以推進協商或索償。實務上不少業主會兩者並行——一邊等候官方調查，一邊先取得自己的技術證據。

建議的處理次序

  1. 拍照及記錄滲漏情況與日期，保留證據
  2. 以書面通知樓上業主或管理處（保留紀錄）
  3. 安排專業勘察，取得報告
  4. 按報告結果協商維修安排
  5. 如協商不成，循官方或民事途徑跟進

本文為一般說明，不構成法律意見。

Where the water usually comes from

  • Failed waterproofing in the flat above — bathrooms, kitchens and balconies especially
  • Leaking supply or drainage pipes, possibly buried in the slab or walls
  • Common pipework — the owners' corporation's responsibility, not an individual owner's
  • External wall or window joint failure, tracking through the structure
  • Air-conditioning condensate drainage
  • Cracking in the slab combined with standing water above

Different sources mean different responsible parties — which is precisely why investigation matters.

How professional investigation works

  • Moisture testing — mapping moisture content to define extent and direction
  • Infrared thermography — locating damp zones within the structure by temperature difference
  • Colour water testing — dye introduced at suspected sources
  • Ponding and isolation tests — sealing or filling in stages to eliminate possibilities
  • Pipe inspection — endoscopic examination where needed
Why "just open it up and look" rarely pays

Opening walls without testing causes damage even when the source is found — and repeated damage when it is not. A structured test sequence exists to locate the likely source with the least destruction.

What the report is for

Beyond identifying the source and recommending repair, a full report provides objective evidence for negotiation and recovery. In practice it supports:

  • Discussion with the upstairs owner or the owners' corporation
  • Technical submissions to the government's joint office
  • Insurance claims
  • Civil claims or Small Claims Tribunal proceedings

Official channel versus private investigation

The government's Joint Office handles seepage complaints amounting to a statutory nuisance. It is free but has limits: a set investigation sequence, waiting times, and not every case qualifies as a nuisance in law.

Private investigation is faster, self-directed and produces a complete report immediately, which can be used to press negotiation or a claim. Many owners run both in parallel.

Suggested sequence

  1. Photograph and date the evidence
  2. Notify the upstairs owner or management in writing, keeping records
  3. Commission a professional investigation and obtain the report
  4. Negotiate repairs on the basis of the findings
  5. If negotiation fails, pursue the official or civil route

General guidance only, not legal advice.
