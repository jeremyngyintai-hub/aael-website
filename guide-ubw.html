僭建物一定要拆嗎？違例建築工程的三條出路Do Unauthorised Building Works Always Have to Come Down?

躍昇建築事務顧問有限公司　·　更新於 2026年7月Ascend Architecture & Engineering Ltd　·　Updated July 2026

很多業主收到清拆令的第一個反應，是以為必須立即把整個結構拆掉。實際上，違例建築工程在法例下可分為三類處理方式，而分辨屬於哪一類，往往決定了成本相差數以倍計。The first reaction to a removal order is usually that everything must come down. In fact unauthorised works fall into three categories, and identifying which one you are dealing with can change the cost by a multiple.

三類處理方式

一、屬「不涉及重大安全風險」而獲寬免執法

屋宇署對違例建築工程採取分級執法。部分規模細小、不影響結構及消防安全的工程（例如若干符合尺寸規定的簷篷、晾衣架、小型花架），在署方的執法政策下可能不會被優先取締。留意：這是執法優次的問題，不等於合法。物業轉讓時，買方律師及銀行仍可能提出，亦不排除日後署方改變政策或因投訴而處理。

二、可透過法定途徑規範化

部分工程本身並非不可接受，只是「未經批准」。若其設計符合現行《建築物條例》及規例的要求，可由認可人士（A.P.）及註冊結構工程師（R.S.E.）評估後，補交圖則申請批准及同意展開工程，取得合法地位。常見例子包括室內間隔改動、部分平台或天台結構、加建露台或圍封等，視乎具體設計、樓宇圖則及所在位置。

能否規範化的關鍵

不是「拆或不拆」的態度問題，而是三個技術問題：結構是否安全（能否通過結構計算）、有否影響走火通道及消防要求、有否違反地契或總樓面面積限制。三項有一項不通過，規範化便不可行。

三、必須清拆

若工程涉及結構安全風險、阻塞走火通道、違反地契條款，或本身無法符合現行規例（例如超出可建範圍、影響採光通風），則沒有規範化空間，須按命令清拆及還原。

收到清拆令後的正確次序

  1. 不要先動工——無論拆或改，未經評估就施工，可能破壞可規範化的機會，或造成新的結構問題。
  2. 委任專業人士評估——由認可人士及註冊結構工程師檢視現況、樓宇批准圖則及命令內容，判斷屬於哪一類。
  3. 選定路線並執行——規範化則入則申請；清拆則安排註冊承建商施工並由專業人士監督。
  4. 提交完工文件解除命令——命令須正式解除，否則會一直留在土地註冊處記錄上。

與買賣及按揭的關係

僭建對物業交易的影響，往往比業主預期大。買方律師查冊時會發現已註冊的命令；銀行估價及批核按揭亦會考慮。因此在放盤前處理妥當，通常比在交易期間匆忙應付更省成本，議價上亦不至於處於下風。

與簡樸房認證的關係

持有分間單位的業主須特別留意：未遵辦的清拆令一般會阻礙簡樸房認證獲批。若計劃申請認證，僭建問題應在同一個評估中一併處理，而非分開兩次委聘、兩次施工。

Three possible outcomes

1. Works subject to lower enforcement priority

The Buildings Department enforces against unauthorised works on a graded basis. Minor items that do not affect structural or fire safety — certain compliant canopies, drying racks, small trellises — may not be prioritised for enforcement. Note this is a matter of enforcement priority, not legality. On a sale, the buyer's solicitors and the bank may still raise it, and policy or a complaint can change the position.

2. Works capable of regularisation

Some works are not objectionable in themselves — they were simply never approved. Where the design meets the current Buildings Ordinance and regulations, an Authorized Person and Registered Structural Engineer can assess and submit plans for approval and consent, giving the works lawful status. Internal layout changes, certain podium or roof structures, and some enclosures fall into this category depending on design, approved plans and location.

What determines whether regularisation is possible

Not attitude, but three technical questions: is the structure safe (can it pass structural calculation); does it affect means of escape and fire requirements; does it breach the lease or gross floor area limits. Fail any one and regularisation is off the table.

3. Works that must be removed

Where works create structural risk, obstruct escape routes, breach lease conditions or simply cannot comply with current regulations, there is no room to regularise and the order must be complied with by removal and reinstatement.

The right sequence after a removal order

  1. Do not start work first — demolishing or altering before assessment can destroy the chance of regularisation or create new structural problems.
  2. Appoint professionals to assess — an Authorized Person and Registered Structural Engineer review the works, the approved plans and the order to determine the category.
  3. Choose the route and execute — submission for regularisation, or a registered contractor with professional supervision for removal.
  4. File completion documents to discharge the order — otherwise the registration remains against the title.

Effect on sale and mortgage

Unauthorised works affect transactions more than owners expect. Registered orders surface on the buyer's title search, and banks take them into account on valuation and mortgage approval. Dealing with the issue before listing is usually cheaper than scrambling mid-transaction, and leaves the owner in a stronger negotiating position.

Connection with BHU certification

Owners of subdivided units should note that an outstanding removal order will generally block Basic Housing Unit certification. If certification is planned, the unauthorised works should be dealt with in the same assessment — not through two separate appointments and two rounds of works.
