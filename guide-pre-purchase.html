買樓前檢驗 PRE-PURCHASE

二手樓買樓前檢驗：簽臨約之前應該查甚麼Pre-purchase Inspection: What to Check Before You Sign

躍昇建築事務顧問有限公司　·　更新於 2026年7月Ascend Architecture & Engineering Ltd　·　Updated July 2026

買樓是一生中最大的一筆支出，卻往往是決定得最快的一筆。臨時買賣合約一經簽署，樓宇本身的問題便隨物業一併過戶——包括賣方沒有說、甚至自己也不知道的問題。Buying a flat is the largest purchase most people make, and often the fastest decision. Once the provisional agreement is signed, the building's problems transfer with the property — including those the seller never mentioned, and some the seller never knew.

五類必查項目

一、僭建及未經批准的改動

這是二手樓最常見、亦最昂貴的意外。天台屋、平台搭建、露台圍封、間隔改動——若與批准圖則不符，即屬違例建築工程。後果由新業主承擔：可能收到清拆令、影響按揭成數，甚至令銀行拒絕批出貸款。

賣方「照現狀出售」不等於免責

合約條款可以處理買賣雙方之間的責任，但不能免除屋宇署對物業採取行動。命令是針對物業及其擁有人發出的——過戶之後，那個擁有人就是您。

二、結構改動

過往裝修有否拆除或開鑿主力牆？有否在樓板加建重物、加設夾層？這些改動若未經入則批准，除了違例，更可能影響結構安全。牆身出現斜向裂縫、地板不平、門框變形，都是值得追查的訊號。

三、滲漏

檢查天花、牆角、廁所及廚房牆身有否水漬、發霉、批盪起泡或油漆變色。留意：賣方在放盤前重新油漆是常見做法，新油漆之下可能掩蓋長期滲漏痕跡。潮濕氣味比外觀更誠實。

四、法定命令及紀錄

樓宇或單位是否尚有未遵辦的屋宇署命令、消防處指示或渠務通知？這些會在土地註冊處留下紀錄，亦會影響按揭及日後轉售。委聘專業人士查冊及查核記錄，比事後補救便宜得多。

五、公用部分及大廈狀況

買的是單位，承擔的卻是整幢樓。外牆狀況、公用喉管、天台防水、有否即將展開的大維修——這些都會轉化為日後的管理費及攤分費用。若大廈剛收到強制驗樓通知而尚未展開工程，未來的維修分攤額可能相當可觀。

發現問題之後，怎樣用

檢驗的價值不只是「買或不買」，更在於把發現轉化為議價依據或條件：

  • 要求賣方在成交前處理（例如自費清拆僭建、解除命令）
  • 按預計修葺費用議價
  • 在合約加入相關條件
  • 若問題嚴重且無法解決，及早退出——這也是檢驗的回報

時機

最理想是在簽署臨時買賣合約之前完成檢驗。市況緊張時未必允許，但即使只有一次帶專業人士到場的機會，已足以識別上述五類問題中最嚴重的部分。

Five things to check

1. Unauthorised building works

The most common and most expensive surprise. Rooftop structures, podium additions, balcony enclosures, altered layouts — anything inconsistent with the approved plans is unauthorised. The consequence transfers to the buyer: removal orders, reduced mortgage lending, sometimes refusal to lend at all.

"Sold as seen" is not immunity

Contract terms allocate risk between buyer and seller. They do not stop the Buildings Department acting against the property. Orders attach to the property and its owner — after completion, that owner is you.

2. Structural alterations

Have structural walls been removed or cut into? Has loading been added, or a mezzanine installed? Unapproved structural work is both an offence and a safety question. Diagonal cracking, uneven floors and distorted door frames are worth investigating.

3. Water seepage

Check ceilings, wall junctions, bathroom and kitchen walls for staining, mould, blistering plaster or discoloured paint. Repainting before listing is routine, and fresh paint hides history — smell is often more honest than appearance.

4. Statutory orders and records

Are there outstanding Buildings Department orders, fire service directions or drainage notices? These are registered against the title and affect mortgage and resale. Searching the records beforehand is far cheaper than dealing with them afterwards.

5. Common parts and the building as a whole

You buy a flat but you inherit a building. External wall condition, common pipework, roof waterproofing, and any major repair programme about to start all convert into future management charges and contributions. A building that has received an MBIS notice but not yet carried out works carries a real future liability.

Turning findings into leverage

  • Require the seller to deal with items before completion
  • Negotiate on the basis of estimated rectification cost
  • Build conditions into the agreement
  • Walk away early where the problem cannot be solved — that too is a return on the inspection

Timing

Ideally the inspection happens before the provisional agreement is signed. Competitive markets do not always allow it, but even a single supervised visit will identify the most serious of the five categories above.
