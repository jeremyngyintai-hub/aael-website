違例出租的後果：執法安排、罰則與認證名單公開Enforcement, Penalties and the Public Register

躍昇建築事務顧問有限公司　·　更新於 2026年7月Ascend Architecture & Engineering Ltd　·　Updated July 2026

規管制度真正的壓力，不在登記，而在登記之後。由執法開始的一日起，未登記或未獲認證而出租，屬刑事罪行；而政府亦計劃公開已認證單位的名單——屆時「有冇認證」會直接寫在租客面前。The pressure in this regime does not sit at registration; it sits after it. From the enforcement date, letting an unregistered or uncertified unit is a criminal offence — and the government plans to publish the list of certified units, putting the answer directly in front of tenants.

執法由何時開始

登記期為十二個月，由二〇二六年三月一日起。登記期結束後，當局將就未有有效登記及未獲簡樸房認證而出租的分間單位展開執法。已登記的單位，可在其後的寬限期內進行改造並申請認證。

據當局公布的安排，執法會以風險為本的模式陸續進行，而非一次過大規模掃蕩；當局亦表示正研究設立舉報機制。這一點值得業主留意：風險為本的意思，是問題愈明顯的單位愈早被處理。

罰則的結構

非法出租未有有效登記的分間單位，或已登記但未獲認證而在寬限期後出租，均屬刑事罪行。罰則按檢控程序分為兩級：

  • 循簡易程序定罪——罰款及監禁
  • 循公訴程序定罪——罰款及監禁刑期較重

兩者共通的一點特別值得注意：可就罪行持續的每一日另處罰款。換言之，罰則並非一次性，而是隨違例持續而累加。

為何「每日計算」改變了風險性質

一次性罰款，理論上可以當作營運成本計算。按日累加的罰款則不同——拖得愈久，數字愈大，而且沒有上限保護。這使得「觀望一下再算」成為所有選項中最昂貴的一個。

具體罰則金額以《簡樸房條例》為準，建議直接參閱房屋局簡樸房專題網站的最新公布。

認證名單公開：由風險變成機會

當局表示會設立網站，公布已登記及已取得認證的簡樸房地址，並指租戶亦有責任了解所住單位的情況。

對業主而言，這件事有兩面：

  • 風險面——未認證的單位，租客一查即知。在資訊透明的市場，未認證等於難以招租，甚至現有租客主動遷出。
  • 機會面——已認證的單位，公開名單就是最有力的招租憑證。合規本身變成賣點，而愈早取得，愈早享有這個優勢期。

換句話說，認證的價值不止於「避免被罰」，而是在市場上與未認證單位分開定價。

業主應如何準備

  1. 先確認資格——樓宇類別、位置、業權文件。不合資格的單位，及早考慮其他出路，總好過投入改造費用之後才發現。
  2. 盡早完成登記——登記是取得寬限期的唯一途徑，期限不設延展。
  3. 及早做全面評估——包括大廈層面的問題，因為這類問題解決得最慢。
  4. 編定工程與資金時間表——寬限期看似充裕，但公用事業申請、法定入則及施工檔期都需要時間。
  5. 保留所有文件——登記、核證報告、認證及相關往來，日後續期及租務糾紛時都用得上。

本文為一般說明，不構成法律意見。實際罰則及執法安排以《簡樸房條例》及房屋局最新公布為準。

When enforcement begins

The registration period runs for twelve months from 1 March 2026. After it closes, enforcement follows against subdivided units let without valid registration and without certification. Registered units may carry out works and apply for certification during the subsequent grace period.

Enforcement is to proceed on a risk-based basis rather than by mass sweep, and the authorities have indicated a reporting mechanism is under study. Risk-based means the most visibly problematic units are dealt with first.

How the penalties are structured

Letting an unregistered subdivided unit, or letting a registered but uncertified unit after the grace period, is a criminal offence. Penalties run at two levels — summary conviction, and conviction on indictment with heavier fines and imprisonment.

Both share one feature worth attention: a further fine for each day the offence continues. The penalty is not a one-off; it accumulates.

Why a daily penalty changes the risk

A single fine can, in theory, be treated as a cost of doing business. A daily accruing fine cannot. The longer the delay, the larger the figure, with no ceiling to shelter behind — which makes "wait and see" the most expensive option available.

Specific penalty levels are as set out in the Ordinance; refer to the Housing Bureau's Basic Housing Unit website for current figures.

The public register: risk and opportunity

The authorities have indicated a website will publish the addresses of registered and certified units, noting that tenants also bear responsibility for knowing the status of where they live.

  • The risk — an uncertified unit is visible to any prospective tenant with a browser. In a transparent market, uncertified means hard to let, and may prompt existing tenants to leave.
  • The opportunity — for certified units, the register is the strongest letting credential there is. Compliance becomes a selling point, and the earlier it is obtained, the longer that advantage lasts.

Certification is therefore not only about avoiding penalties; it is about being priced differently from uncertified stock.

How owners should prepare

  1. Confirm eligibility first — building type, location, ownership documents.
  2. Register early — registration is the only route to the grace period, and the deadline does not extend.
  3. Assess comprehensively and early, including building-level issues, which take longest to resolve.
  4. Programme the works and the money — utility applications, statutory submissions and construction slots all consume time.
  5. Keep every document — registration, certification report, the certificate itself and correspondence will matter at renewal and in any tenancy dispute.
