簡樸房認證申請為何會被拒絕？單位合格不等於批得到Why BHU Certification Applications Are Refused

躍昇建築事務顧問有限公司　·　更新於 2026年7月Ascend Architecture & Engineering Ltd　·　Updated July 2026

很多業主以為，只要單位改到合標準，認證便是時間問題。實情是：房屋局在審批時，會一併考慮分間單位所處整幢建築物的情況。單位十足合格，仍然可以被拒。Owners often assume that once the unit meets the standards, certification is a formality. In fact the Housing Bureau also considers the condition of the whole building. A fully compliant unit can still be refused.

一、大廈公用部分存在危險狀況

這是最容易被忽略、也最難補救的一項。若建築物的公用部分存在對生命或財產構成迫切危險或風險的情況——例如有嚴重問題的排水渠、結構性裂縫、僭建物阻塞逃生通道——即使您的分間單位完全符合最低標準，當局亦可拒絕認證申請。

為何這一項最傷

單位內部的問題，您自己可以出錢解決。大廈公用部分的問題，往往需要業主立案法團決議、全體業主分攤、甚至召開業主大會——時間以月甚至年計。若到遞交申請時才發現，寬限期可能已所餘無幾。

這也是我們一再強調「評估要看整幢樓，不能只看單位」的原因。裝修方案做得再漂亮，也不會令大廈的結構裂縫消失。

二、未遵辦的法定命令、通知或指示

若屋宇署或其他政府部門就分間單位或其所處的樓宇單位發出過法定命令、通知或指示而尚未遵辦，一般須先行處理妥當，方可獲批認證。

常見情況：外牆修葺令、渠務修理通知、清拆令、強制驗樓法定通知。這些不一定針對您的單位，而可能是針對整幢樓——但同樣會擋住您的申請。

三、僭建物

單位內或相關位置的違例建築工程須先處理。特別注意：位於天台、平台、後巷、天井或庭院等位置的僭建物，本身並不符合簡樸房認證的申請資格——即是說，這類分間單位不是「改到合標準就可以」，而是根本不在制度容許之列。

四、樓宇類別不符

簡樸房認證只適用於位處住用建築物，或綜合用途建築物住用部分的樓宇單位。以下均不符合申請資格：

  • 綜合用途建築物的非住用部分
  • 商業大廈
  • 工業大廈
  • 非法構築物（包括寮屋及天台、平台、後巷、天井等的僭建物）
  • 新界豁免管制屋宇（俗稱村屋）任何樓層的處所

不少業主在工廈或村屋單位投放大量改裝費用之後，才發現該類樓宇根本不能申請。這一項應該在花第一分錢之前確認。

五、業權同意文件不足

申請人須為分間單位所處樓宇單位的唯一擁有人，或已取得所有擁有人書面同意的人士。若屬共同擁有，須提交其餘所有共同擁有人的書面同意；若申請人並非擁有人（例如二房東、營運者），則須提交所有擁有人的書面同意。

實務上，共有業權、繼承中的物業、或業主身處海外，都可能令這一步耗時遠超預期。宜及早處理。

六、核證報告本身的問題

核證報告須由指明專業人士提交，確認單位符合居住環境最低標準，以及所有相關法定命令均已履行。報告內容不完整、圖則與現場不符、或核證範圍未涵蓋所有必要項目，同樣會導致申請被延誤或退回。

正確的做法：先做「可批性」評估

與其先裝修、後申請，合理次序應該倒過來：

  1. 確認樓宇類別及申請資格——這一關不過，其餘都不用談。
  2. 查核法定命令及大廈狀況——包括公用部分，及可能影響審批的整幢樓層面問題。
  3. 確認業權及同意文件可以取得。
  4. 然後才評估單位需要哪些改造，並編定工程及預算。
一句話總結

裝修公司可以令您的單位合標準；能否取得認證，取決於整幢樓、法定紀錄與業權文件。這三樣，都不是裝修解決得到的。

本文為一般說明，實際要求以《簡樸房條例》及房屋局最新公布為準。

1. Dangerous conditions in the building's common parts

The most overlooked and hardest to fix. Where common parts present an imminent danger or risk to life or property — seriously defective drains, structural cracking, unauthorised works obstructing escape routes — the application may be refused even though your unit fully meets the standards.

Why this one hurts

Problems inside the unit you can pay to fix. Problems in the common parts usually need an owners' corporation resolution, contributions from all owners, and a general meeting — months, sometimes years. Discovered at application stage, there may be little grace period left.

2. Outstanding statutory orders, notices or directions

Where the Buildings Department or another authority has issued an order, notice or direction affecting the unit or the building unit and it has not been complied with, that generally has to be resolved before certification. These often target the building rather than your flat — and still block your application.

3. Unauthorised building works

Unauthorised works must be dealt with. Note particularly that structures on rooftops, podiums, rear lanes, light wells or yards are not eligible for certification at all — such units are not a matter of bringing them up to standard; they fall outside the scheme.

4. The building type does not qualify

Certification applies only to units in residential buildings, or the residential part of composite buildings. Not eligible: non-residential parts of composite buildings; commercial buildings; industrial buildings; illegal structures including squatter huts and rooftop, podium, rear lane or light well structures; and premises on any floor of New Territories Exempted Houses.

Owners sometimes spend heavily converting industrial or village house units before discovering the building type can never qualify. Confirm this before spending anything.

5. Insufficient ownership consent

The applicant must be the sole owner of the building unit, or hold written consent from all owners. Co-ownership, estates under administration and overseas owners routinely take far longer than expected — start early.

6. Problems with the certification report itself

The report must be submitted by a specified professional confirming compliance with the minimum standards and that relevant statutory orders have been complied with. An incomplete report, drawings inconsistent with site conditions, or a scope that misses required items will delay or return the application.

The right approach: test approvability first

  1. Confirm building type and eligibility — nothing else matters if this fails.
  2. Check statutory records and building condition, including common parts.
  3. Confirm ownership consents can actually be obtained.
  4. Only then assess what the unit needs and set the programme and budget.
In one line

A renovation contractor can make your unit compliant. Whether it gets certified depends on the building, the statutory record and the ownership documents — none of which renovation fixes.

General guidance only; requirements are as set out in the Ordinance and current Housing Bureau publications.
