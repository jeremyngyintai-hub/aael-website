業主立案法團大維修：招標、監督與常見陷阱Major Repairs for Owners' Corporations: Tendering, Supervision and Pitfalls

躍昇建築事務顧問有限公司　·　更新於 2026年7月Ascend Architecture & Engineering Ltd　·　Updated July 2026

樓宇大維修的爭議，很少源於工程技術，多數源於程序。程序做得對，價格與質素自然有制衡；程序有瑕疵，即使工程做得好，日後仍可能被質疑。Disputes over major building repairs rarely start with the engineering. They start with procedure. Get the procedure right and price and quality look after themselves; get it wrong and even good work invites challenge.

大維修通常從哪裡開始

觸發樓宇大維修的原因主要有三類：收到強制驗樓法定通知、樓宇出現明顯失修或安全問題，或法團按樓宇狀況主動籌劃。無論哪一種，第一步都應該是先掌握樓宇實際狀況，而非先問價錢。沒有勘察報告就招標，等於請承建商各自猜測工程範圍——報價無從比較，日後亦容易出現大量「額外工程」。

顧問與承建商：角色必須分開

為何不應由承建商一手包辦

承建商的利益在於工程量，顧問的利益應在於代表業主把關。若由同一方既定範圍又施工又驗收，制衡便消失。這是大維修爭議中最常見的結構性問題。

合理的分工是：顧問（測量師／工程師）負責勘察、擬定工程範圍及規格、協助招標、施工期間監督及驗收；承建商負責施工。兩者由法團分別委聘，各自向法團負責。

法定招標程序

《建築物管理條例》就法團採購訂有程序要求，涉及一定金額或按管理費比例的合約須經公開招標，並須在業主大會上通過。實務要點包括：

  • 招標文件須清楚界定工程範圍、規格及工期
  • 投標須密封，於指定日期公開開標
  • 標書分析應由顧問整理，向業主清楚說明價格差異的原因
  • 決標須依大會決議，並保留完整紀錄

程序上的瑕疵，日後可能成為個別業主提出質疑甚至法律挑戰的理由，因此紀錄的完整性與過程的透明度同樣重要。

資助計劃

市區重建局及相關機構設有多項樓宇維修資助計劃，適用於符合條件的舊樓及業主。申請通常有時間及程序要求，並須配合工程進度。若有意申請，應在籌備初期一併規劃，而非工程開展後才處理。

顧問應提供的價值

合資格的顧問除了技術判斷，更重要的是為法團承擔可問責的把關角色：範圍是否合理、價格是否相稱、工程是否按規格完成、業主的錢是否用得其所。對法團委員而言，這亦是一種保障——決策有專業依據，日後面對業主質詢時有據可依。

本文為一般說明，不構成法律意見。實際程序須按《建築物管理條例》及公契條款辦理。

Where major repairs usually begin

Three triggers: an MBIS statutory notice, visible deterioration or safety concerns, or the corporation planning ahead. In every case the first step should be establishing the building's actual condition, not asking for prices. Tendering without a condition survey asks contractors to guess the scope — bids become incomparable and variations proliferate.

Consultant and contractor: keep the roles apart

Why one party should not do everything

A contractor's interest lies in volume of work; a consultant's should lie in protecting the owners. Where the same party defines the scope, builds it and signs it off, the check disappears. This is the most common structural cause of disputes.

The workable division: the consultant surveys, defines scope and specification, assists with tendering, supervises and certifies; the contractor builds. Both are appointed by, and answer to, the corporation.

Statutory tendering

The Building Management Ordinance sets procurement requirements for corporations; contracts above certain thresholds require open tender and approval at a general meeting. Practical points:

  • Tender documents must define scope, specification and programme clearly
  • Bids sealed and opened publicly on the appointed date
  • Tender analysis prepared by the consultant, explaining price differences to owners
  • Award by resolution, with complete records retained

Procedural defects give individual owners grounds to challenge later, so transparency and record-keeping matter as much as price.

Subsidy schemes

The Urban Renewal Authority and related bodies operate building repair subsidy schemes for eligible buildings and owners. Applications carry timing and procedural conditions tied to works progress, so they should be planned at the outset rather than retrofitted.

What a consultant should actually be worth

Beyond technical judgement, the consultant carries an accountable gatekeeping role: is the scope justified, the price proportionate, the work built to specification, the owners' money well spent. For committee members that is also protection — decisions rest on professional grounds that can be explained when owners ask.
