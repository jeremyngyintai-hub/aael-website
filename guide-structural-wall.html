拆牆 · 結構 STRUCTURE

裝修拆牆之前：如何分辨主力牆與須辦的手續Before You Remove a Wall: Structural Walls and What the Law Requires

躍昇建築事務顧問有限公司　·　更新於 2026年7月Ascend Architecture & Engineering Ltd　·　Updated July 2026

「呢幅牆拆得㗎，唔係主力牆。」——裝修工程中最常聽見、也最沒有依據的一句話。分辨主力牆不能靠敲、靠經驗、靠肉眼，只能靠圖則與結構計算。"That one's fine, it's not structural." It is the most frequently heard sentence on a renovation site and the least evidenced. Structural walls are not identified by tapping, experience or eye — only by drawings and calculation.

為甚麼不能靠敲牆判斷

坊間常說「敲落去實心就係主力牆」。這並不可靠：混凝土牆固然結實，但部分磚牆或加厚間隔牆敲起來同樣沉實；相反，某些結構牆在裝修飾面之下，敲擊聲亦未必如預期。

唯一可靠的方法

查閱樓宇的批准圖則（結構圖），對照現場，由註冊結構工程師確認。圖則顯示的是設計意圖與結構體系——哪些構件承受荷載、荷載如何傳遞——這是敲牆永遠得不到的資訊。

拆錯牆的後果

  • 結構風險——荷載失去傳遞路徑，可能導致上層樓板下陷、牆身裂縫，嚴重者影響整體結構安全。
  • 法律後果——未經批准的結構改動屬違例建築工程，可收到清拆令，須還原。
  • 財務後果——還原工程往往比原本的裝修更貴；命令登記於土地註冊處，影響按揭及轉售。
  • 保險——因未經批准改動導致的損失，保險未必受保。

哪些改動需要辦手續

一般而言，涉及以下情況便須循法定途徑處理（視具體情況屬小型工程或正式入則）：

  • 拆除、開鑿或削薄任何結構牆、樑、柱、樓板
  • 在結構構件上開孔（例如穿喉、開門洞）
  • 加建夾層、增加樓面荷載
  • 改動或加設影響結構的支承
  • 更改外牆、窗洞尺寸

相反，拆除純粹分隔用途的非結構輕質間隔，通常不涉及結構，但仍可能因涉及消防、走火通道或渠務而有其他要求。

正確次序

  1. 先查圖則——取得樓宇批准圖則，確認結構體系。
  2. 專業評估——由註冊結構工程師判斷擬議改動是否可行，需否補強。
  3. 確定法定路線——屬小型工程或須正式入則，由認可人士協助辦理。
  4. 取得批准後才動工——由適當級別的註冊承建商施工。
  5. 完工申報——按規定提交文件。
給業主的實際建議

在簽署裝修合約之前，就把「結構改動需經註冊結構工程師確認及辦妥法定手續」寫入條款，並訂明費用由誰承擔。事後才發現要停工補辦，時間與金錢的損失通常遠高於事前查證。

本文為一般說明，個別情況須按批准圖則及專業評估處理。

Why tapping the wall proves nothing

The site wisdom is that a solid sound means a structural wall. It does not hold: concrete walls are solid, but so are some brick and thickened partitions, while finishes can mask the sound of a structural element entirely.

The only reliable method

Obtain the approved plans, compare them with what is on site, and have a Registered Structural Engineer confirm. Drawings show the load path — which elements carry load and how it travels — which no amount of tapping will reveal.

What happens if you get it wrong

  • Structural risk — loads lose their path, producing deflection above, cracking, and in serious cases a genuine safety issue.
  • Legal — unapproved structural alteration is unauthorised building work, attracting a removal order and reinstatement.
  • Financial — reinstatement often costs more than the original works, and the registered order affects mortgage and resale.
  • Insurance — losses arising from unapproved alterations may not be covered.

What needs statutory handling

  • Removing, cutting into or thinning any structural wall, beam, column or slab
  • Forming openings in structural elements for services or doors
  • Adding mezzanines or increasing floor loading
  • Altering or adding structural support
  • Changing external walls or window openings

Removing genuinely non-structural lightweight partitions usually raises no structural issue, though fire, escape route and drainage requirements may still apply.

The right order

  1. Check the plans and establish the structural system.
  2. Professional assessment by a Registered Structural Engineer on feasibility and any strengthening.
  3. Fix the statutory route — minor works or full submission, with an Authorized Person where required.
  4. Start only after approval, using a registered contractor of the appropriate class.
  5. File completion documents as required.
A practical clause for owners

Before signing the renovation contract, write in that structural alterations require confirmation by a Registered Structural Engineer and completion of statutory procedures, and state who bears that cost. Discovering it mid-works costs far more than checking first.

General guidance only; individual cases follow the approved plans and professional assessment.
