指明專業人士 SPECIFIED PROFESSIONAL

簡樸房核證報告由誰簽署？「指明專業人士」資格、查證與委聘Who Signs the BHU Certification Report? The Specified Professional Explained

躍昇建築事務顧問有限公司　·　更新於 2026年7月Ascend Architecture & Engineering Ltd　·　Updated July 2026

申請簡樸房認證，必須附上一份由「指明專業人士」提交的核證報告。這一點看似技術細節，實際上決定了整個項目的成敗——因為代辦、裝修公司與核證專業人士，是三個不同的角色，而只有最後一個能夠簽名。A Basic Housing Unit application must be accompanied by a certification report from a specified professional. It reads like a technical detail; in practice it decides the project — because the agent, the contractor and the certifying professional are three different roles, and only the last one can sign.

誰可以擔任指明專業人士

根據《簡樸房條例》，認證申請須附上由指明專業人士提交的核證報告，確認單位符合居住環境最低標準，並且所有相關的法定命令均已履行。指明專業人士包括以下註冊專業資格：

  • 註冊建築師
  • 註冊專業工程師（相關界別，其後亦擴展至消防及屋宇裝備範疇）
  • 註冊專業測量師（建築測量組別）——R.P.S.(BS)
關鍵一點

業主須自行委聘指明專業人士。這意味著：即使您委託了代辦或裝修公司統籌，核證這一環仍然要有一位持相關註冊資格的專業人士簽署——而該專業人士對報告內容負專業責任。

三個角色，不要混淆

角色做甚麼能否簽核證報告
代辦／顧問公司統籌、文件處理、聯絡否（除非本身持有指明資格）
裝修／工程公司設計及施工改造否（除非本身持有指明資格）
指明專業人士檢視、量度、核證是

市場上不少「一站式」服務，實際結構是由前兩者承接，再向外委聘第三者簽署。這種安排本身沒有問題，但業主應該清楚知道：簽名的人是誰、由誰對報告負責、費用是否已包含在報價之內。

委聘之前應確認甚麼

  1. 對方持有哪一項指明資格——註冊建築師、註冊專業工程師，還是註冊專業測量師（建築測量組別）。
  2. 資格是公司內部持有，還是外聘——若是外聘，實際簽署者是誰？何時介入？
  3. 核證範圍是否包含法定命令的核實——核證報告須確認相關法定命令已履行，這需要查冊及跟進，並非只量度單位。
  4. 若發現障礙，由誰處理——例如需要處理僭建、結構問題或屋宇署命令時，對方能否承接，還是要您另找他人。
  5. 五年後續期由誰跟進——續期須再次核證，原始紀錄在誰手上會影響日後成本。

如何查證專業資格

香港的專業註冊制度是公開的：

  • 認可人士、註冊結構工程師、註冊檢驗人員——可於屋宇署的公開名冊查核
  • 註冊專業測量師——可向測量師註冊管理局查核；香港測量師學會建築測量組亦有公布「指明專業人士」的註冊專業測量師（建築測量組別）名單
  • 註冊建築師、註冊專業工程師——可向相關註冊管理局查核

查證需時不足十分鐘，卻是委聘前最值得做的一步。若對方對「由誰簽名」含糊其詞，這本身就是一個訊號。

為何有人會找不到願意核證的專業人士

核證報告帶有專業責任：簽署者須確認單位符合標準，並且相關法定命令已履行。若樓宇本身狀況複雜——有未解除的命令、公用部分問題、或結構疑問——單純的核證方可能不願承接，因為問題不在其能力或委任範圍之內。

這正是實務上最常出現的樽頸：裝修完成，卻找不到人簽名。解決方法不是繼續找核證方，而是先處理令對方卻步的根本問題——而這需要認可人士或註冊結構工程師的介入。

給業主與同業的實際建議

若能在項目開始之前，就由同時持有核證資格與法定處理能力的一方作出評估，可以避免「做完才發現簽不到」這種最昂貴的情況。核證不應該是最後一步才考慮的事，而應該是第一步。

本文為一般說明，實際要求以《簡樸房條例》及房屋局最新公布為準。

Who qualifies as a specified professional

Under the Ordinance, an application must be accompanied by a certification report from a specified professional confirming that the unit meets the minimum living-environment standards and that relevant statutory orders have been complied with. Those professionals include:

  • Registered Architects
  • Registered Professional Engineers in relevant disciplines, later extended to fire and building services
  • Registered Professional Surveyors (Building Surveying) — R.P.S.(BS)
The key point

The owner must appoint the specified professional. Even where an agent or contractor coordinates everything, the certification still requires a registered professional to sign — and that professional carries the responsibility for the report.

Three roles, often confused

RoleDoesCan sign?
Agent / consultancyCoordination, documents, liaisonNo, unless itself qualified
ContractorDesign and constructionNo, unless itself qualified
Specified professionalInspects, measures, certifiesYes

Many "one-stop" offerings are in fact the first two buying in the third. There is nothing wrong with that — but owners should know who signs, who carries the responsibility, and whether that fee is inside the quotation.

What to establish before appointing

  1. Which qualification they hold.
  2. Whether it is held in-house or bought in — and if bought in, who actually signs and when they get involved.
  3. Whether the scope covers verifying statutory orders, which requires searches and follow-up, not just measuring.
  4. Who deals with obstacles — unauthorised works, structural issues, Buildings Department orders.
  5. Who handles renewal in five years, and who holds the original record.

Verifying the registration

Registration in Hong Kong is public. Authorized Persons, Registered Structural Engineers and Registered Inspectors appear on the Buildings Department's register. Registered Professional Surveyors can be checked with the Surveyors Registration Board, and the HKIS Building Surveying Division publishes a list of R.P.S.(BS) specified professionals. Architects and engineers can be checked with their respective boards.

It takes under ten minutes and is the single most useful check before appointing anyone. Vagueness about who signs is itself informative.

Why some owners cannot find anyone to certify

Certification carries professional responsibility: the signatory confirms compliance and that relevant orders have been complied with. Where the building is complicated — undischarged orders, common-parts problems, structural doubts — a certifier with no remit to fix those things may reasonably decline.

This is the real bottleneck in practice: the works are finished and nobody will sign. The answer is not to keep looking for a certifier, but to resolve what is making them hesitate — which needs an Authorized Person or Registered Structural Engineer.

Practical advice

Having the assessment done at the outset by a party that both certifies and can deal with statutory matters avoids the most expensive outcome of all — finished works that cannot be signed off. Certification should be the first question, not the last.

General guidance only; requirements are as published by the Housing Bureau.
