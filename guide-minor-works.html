法定程序 STATUTORY ROUTES

小型工程還是改建加建？開工前分清法定路線Minor Works or A&A? Getting the Statutory Route Right Before You Start

躍昇建築事務顧問有限公司　·　更新於 2026年7月Ascend Architecture & Engineering Ltd　·　Updated July 2026

同一項裝修工程，走小型工程制度可能兩星期完成申報，走正式入則則可能需時數月。分別不在於工程本身，而在於它在法例下屬於哪一類——而這一步判斷錯誤，後果可以是整個工程需要還原重做。The same renovation can be a two-week minor works filing or a submission taking months. The difference is not the work itself but its classification in law — and getting that wrong can mean undoing everything.

兩條路線的基本分別

小型工程監管制度正式入則（A&A）
適用指定類別及規模的小型工程不屬小型工程類別的建築工程
程序委任訂明建築專業人士／註冊承建商，按級別作事前或事後申報認可人士提交圖則，經屋宇署審批後方可展開工程
時間相對簡短審批需時，一般以月計

三個級別

小型工程按風險及複雜程度分為三級：

  • 第I級——規模及風險較高，須由訂明建築專業人士（如認可人士或註冊結構工程師）及訂明註冊承建商負責，並於工程展開前作出申報。
  • 第II級——中等，同樣須專業人員參與，申報要求較第I級簡化。
  • 第III級——規模最小、風險最低，可由訂明註冊承建商在工程完成後作出申報。
常見誤解

「小型工程」不等於「小工程可以自己做」。制度指的是指定項目類別，而非工程金額或面積大小。不在類別內的工程，無論多小，都不能循小型工程途徑處理；相反，某些看似大型的項目反而屬於制度範圍內。

選錯路線的後果

若本應入則的工程當作小型工程處理，或屬第I級卻按第III級申報，工程本身即成為違例建築工程。後果可能包括：

  • 收到屋宇署命令，須清拆或補辦手續
  • 已完成的裝修須局部拆除以配合結構評估或補交圖則
  • 物業轉讓時被買方或銀行提出
  • 若涉及牌照申請（例如食肆），可能直接導致申請受阻

甚麼時候應該先問專業意見

以下情況建議在報價、簽約及動工之前先確認路線：

  • 涉及拆除或開鑿任何牆體，尤其未能確定是否主力牆
  • 更改廚廁位置、增加或移動排水管
  • 加建、圍封、搭建平台或天台結構
  • 更改外牆、窗戶尺寸或開孔
  • 樓面加建夾層或改變用途
  • 商業處所裝修，尤其其後需要申請牌照

裝修公司熟悉施工，但法定路線的判斷及申報責任，須由持相關資格的專業人士承擔。及早確認，通常只需一次現場檢視與圖則查核，成本遠低於事後補救。

本文為一般說明，實際分類以《建築物條例》及屋宇署最新指引為準。

The basic difference

Minor Works Control SystemFull submission (A&A)
Applies toSpecified categories and scales of minor worksBuilding works outside the minor works categories
ProcessAppoint prescribed building professional / registered contractor; notification before or after works by classAuthorized Person submits plans; approval required before commencement
TimeComparatively shortApproval measured in months

The three classes

  • Class I — higher scale and risk; requires a prescribed building professional (such as an Authorized Person or Registered Structural Engineer) and prescribed registered contractor, with notification before works commence.
  • Class II — intermediate; professional involvement required, with simplified notification.
  • Class III — smallest scale and lowest risk; may be notified by a prescribed registered contractor after completion.
A common misunderstanding

"Minor works" does not mean "small enough to do informally". The system defines specified categories of work, not a value or area threshold. Work outside those categories cannot use the route however small; conversely some apparently substantial items do fall within it.

What happens if the route is wrong

  • A Buildings Department order requiring removal or retrospective submission
  • Completed finishes opened up again for structural assessment or plan submission
  • Issues raised by buyers or banks on a future sale
  • Where a licence application follows — a restaurant, for instance — the application itself can stall

When to take advice first

  • Removing or cutting into any wall, especially where structural status is unclear
  • Relocating kitchens or bathrooms, adding or moving drainage
  • Additions, enclosures, platforms or roof structures
  • Altering external walls, window sizes or openings
  • Adding mezzanines or changing use
  • Commercial fit-outs, particularly where a licence application will follow

Contractors know construction; classification and notification responsibility sits with qualified professionals. Confirming early usually takes one site visit and a plan check — far cheaper than remedying afterwards.

General guidance only; classification follows the Buildings Ordinance and current Buildings Department guidance.
