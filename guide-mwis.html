強制驗窗計劃（MWIS）：業主與租戶各自要做甚麼Mandatory Window Inspection Scheme: Who Does What

躍昇建築事務顧問有限公司　·　更新於 2026年7月Ascend Architecture & Engineering Ltd　·　Updated July 2026

強制驗窗常被誤以為是強制驗樓的一部分。兩者確實常同時發出，但範圍、負責人員及責任分工都不一樣——尤其在單位內窗戶的部分，業主與租戶之間最常出現爭議。Window inspection is often assumed to be part of the building inspection scheme. The two frequently arrive together, but differ in scope, in who carries them out, and in who is responsible — particularly for windows inside a let unit.

檢驗範圍

強制驗窗計劃針對樓齡達指定年期的私人樓宇，檢驗範圍包括樓宇公用部分及各單位內的窗戶。這一點與強制驗樓不同——驗樓集中於公用部分、外牆及伸出物，驗窗則會進入單位之內。

由誰執行

檢驗須由屋宇署名冊上的合資格人士進行；如需修葺，則由註冊小型工程承建商（適當級別）施工。檢驗完成後須向屋宇署提交報告。

業主與租戶的分工

最常見的爭議

單位內窗戶的檢驗及修葺責任，一般屬業主；但租戶須配合安排入屋檢驗的時間。若租戶拒絕配合，業主可能因無法完成檢驗而面對法律後果，因此租約條款及及早溝通十分重要。

實務建議：出租單位的業主，收到通知後應盡早以書面通知租戶，說明法定要求、預計時間及安排，並保留紀錄。管理處協調的統一安排，通常比逐戶自行約時間有效率。

常見的修葺項目

  • 鉸鏈、窗鎖鏽蝕或鬆脫
  • 固定窗框的螺絲或膨脹螺栓鏽蝕
  • 窗框與牆身之間的密封膠老化、開裂
  • 鋁窗料變形、玻璃鬆動
  • 窗戶未能穩固關上或開關時異常鬆動

不少項目屬小型維修，但涉及安全——高層窗戶鬆脫墜落的後果嚴重，這正是計劃存在的原因。

時限與後果

法定通知會列明遵辦期限。不遵辦屬刑事罪行，可被檢控及罰款；屋宇署亦可安排代為檢驗或修葺，並向業主追討費用。如因合理原因未能如期完成，可申請延期，但須有實際進度支持。

與強制驗樓一併處理的好處

當兩份通知同時發出，把檢驗及修葺一併規劃，通常較分開處理划算：搭棚、進場、協調住戶等成本可以共用，住戶受到的滋擾亦減少一次。這需要在收到通知的初期就作整體規劃，而非各自為政。

本文為一般說明，實際要求以屋宇署發出的通知及最新指引為準。

Scope

The Mandatory Window Inspection Scheme applies to private buildings of a specified age and covers windows in both the common parts and individual units. That differs from building inspection, which concentrates on common parts, external walls and projections; window inspection goes inside the flat.

Who carries it out

Inspection must be by a qualified person on the Buildings Department register, with repairs by a registered minor works contractor of the appropriate class. A report is filed with the Department on completion.

Owner and tenant

The usual dispute

Responsibility for inspecting and repairing windows inside a unit generally rests with the owner — but the tenant must allow access. Where a tenant will not cooperate, the owner can face the legal consequences of an incomplete inspection, so tenancy terms and early communication matter.

In practice: write to tenants as soon as the notice arrives, setting out the statutory requirement, expected timing and arrangements, and keep the record. A coordinated programme through the management office usually works better than flat-by-flat appointments.

Common repair items

  • Corroded or loose hinges and locks
  • Corroded fixing screws or anchor bolts
  • Perished or split sealant between frame and wall
  • Distorted aluminium sections, loose glazing
  • Windows that no longer close securely or feel loose in operation

Many are minor repairs, but the safety stake is high — a window falling from height is exactly what the scheme exists to prevent.

Deadlines

The notice states a compliance period. Non-compliance is an offence and may be prosecuted; the Department may also arrange the work and recover costs. Extensions may be sought for good reason, supported by evidence of progress.

Why combine it with building inspection

When both notices arrive together, planning the inspections and repairs as one exercise is usually cheaper: scaffolding, access and resident coordination are shared, and occupants are disturbed once instead of twice. That requires planning at the outset rather than treating them separately.

General guidance only; the operative requirements are those in the notice and current Buildings Department guidance.
