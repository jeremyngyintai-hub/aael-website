簡樸房八項居住環境最低標準：業主自查指南The Eight Minimum Standards for Basic Housing Units

躍昇建築事務顧問有限公司　·　更新於 2026年7月Ascend Architecture & Engineering Ltd　·　Updated July 2026

大部分分間單位不是「完全不合格」，而是在其中一兩項未達標。認清是哪幾項，往往就決定了改造工程是小修還是大動。以下逐項拆解。Most subdivided units do not fail everything — they fail one or two items. Knowing which ones usually decides whether the works are minor or major. Here is each requirement in turn.

一、最低內部樓面面積：8平方米

每個分間單位的內部樓面面積不得少於 8平方米（約86平方呎）。量度以單位內部淨面積計算，牆身厚度不計算在內。

業主自查重點：如單位原為三間分間房而總面積偏緊，改造方案往往是「減間數、加面積」——由三間改為兩間。這在帳面上似乎減少租金收入，但換來的是可合法出租至 2030 年之後的資格，長遠計算通常仍然划算。

二、樓底高度：2.3米

樓底淨高度須達 2.3米。留意：如單位曾加建假天花、包裹冷氣槽或喉管，實際淨高可能已低於標準而業主不自知。舊樓尤其常見。

此項一旦不達標，改造難度較高，因為涉及拆除天花或重新編排喉管走向，有時須同時處理消防及通風設計。這是我們建議及早評估的主要原因之一。

三、獨立水錶及電錶

每個分間單位須設有由 水務署提供的獨立水錶，以及由 電力公司（中電或港燈）提供的獨立電錶。

最常見的誤解

由電工在單位內加裝的「分錶」並不符合要求。條例要求的是由公用事業機構正式提供的獨立計量，涉及正式申請程序及安裝時間，並非現場加個錶就可以。此項是導致認證延誤的頭號原因。

四、獨立廁所及與煮食空間分隔

每個分間單位須設有獨立廁所，並須與煮食位置作適當分隔。共用廁所的安排不符合標準。分隔的具體做法（例如間隔牆、門的設置）須符合《實務守則》的技術要求。

五、窗戶或機械通風

單位須有可開啟的窗戶提供自然通風及採光；若因間隔位置而無窗，則須設置符合要求的機械通風系統。內籠房（無窗房）是常見的不合格情況，改造時須由專業人士評估通風方案是否可行。

六、防火及走火通道

分間工程所使用的間隔材料須具備所需的耐火性能，走火通道須保持暢通及符合闊度要求。這一項與消防安全直接相關，亦是政府執法時的重點。

七、結構安全

不得有違例或影響結構安全的改動，例如拆除或開鑿主力牆、在樓板加建重物。若過往裝修曾動過結構，須由註冊結構工程師評估及作出補救。

八、已遵辦法定命令

如單位或所處樓宇尚有屋宇署或其他政府部門發出的法定命令、通知書或指示未遵辦，一般須先行處理，方可獲批認證。

為何這一項最容易被忽略

業主往往只關注單位內部，卻不知道整幢樓宇層面尚有未清的命令（例如外牆、渠務或僭建命令）。查冊及查核記錄應在評估初期進行，避免工程完成後才發現卡在這一關。

自查之後，下一步

以上八項可作初步自我檢查，但認證所需的核證報告，必須由條例指明的專業人士簽發。實務上，評估的價值不僅是「合格與否」，而是找出最低成本的合規路徑——哪些必須改、哪些其實可以保留、哪些改動反而會觸發額外的入則要求。

本文為概括說明，詳細技術要求以《簡樸房條例》及房屋局發出的《實務守則》為準。

1. Minimum internal floor area: 8 m²

Each subdivided unit must have an internal floor area of at least 8 square metres (about 86 sq ft), measured as net internal area.

A common outcome: a flat currently divided into three tight rooms may need to become two. That looks like lost rent on paper, but it buys the right to let lawfully beyond 2030 — usually the better long-term arithmetic.

2. Ceiling height: 2.3 m

Clear ceiling height must reach 2.3 metres. Watch for false ceilings, boxed-in ducts and pipe runs that have quietly reduced the clear height below standard — very common in older buildings. Failing this item is among the harder problems to fix, as it usually means removing ceilings or re-routing services.

3. Separate water and electricity meters

Each unit needs an individual water meter provided by the Water Supplies Department and an individual electricity meter provided by CLP or HK Electric.

The most common misunderstanding

A sub-meter installed by an electrician inside the flat does not satisfy the requirement. What the Ordinance calls for is metering formally provided by the utility, which involves an application process and lead time. This is the single biggest cause of certification delay.

4. Independent toilet, separated from cooking

Every unit needs its own toilet, properly separated from the cooking area. Shared toilet arrangements do not meet the standard, and the method of separation must follow the technical requirements in the Code of Practice.

5. Window or mechanical ventilation

Units need an openable window for natural light and ventilation; where the layout leaves a room windowless, a compliant mechanical ventilation system is required. Interior rooms with no window are a frequent failure point and need professional assessment.

6. Fire safety and means of escape

Partition materials must achieve the required fire resistance, and escape routes must remain clear and adequately wide. This is a focus area for enforcement.

7. Structural integrity

No unauthorised or unsafe structural alteration — for example removing or cutting into structural walls, or adding heavy loads to slabs. Where past renovations touched the structure, a Registered Structural Engineer must assess and remediate.

8. Outstanding statutory orders cleared

If the unit or the building is subject to an outstanding order, notice or direction from the Buildings Department or another authority, that generally has to be dealt with before certification can be granted.

Why this item is so often missed

Owners look at the flat, not the building. Outstanding orders at building level — external walls, drainage, unauthorised works — surface late and stall applications after the money has been spent. Records should be checked at assessment stage.

After the self-check

These eight points make a reasonable self-check, but the certification report itself must be signed by a specified professional under the Ordinance. In practice the value of a proper assessment is not a pass/fail verdict — it is identifying the lowest-cost compliant route: what must change, what can stay, and which changes would trigger additional statutory submissions.

General guidance only; the full technical requirements are set out in the Ordinance and the Housing Bureau's Code of Practice.
