收到屋宇署命令或通知書怎麼辦？業主應對指南What to Do When You Receive a Buildings Department Order

躍昇建築事務顧問有限公司　·　更新於 2026年7月Ascend Architecture & Engineering Ltd　·　Updated July 2026

屋宇署命令不會因為擱置而消失，反而會累積後果：命令登記在土地註冊處會影響物業轉讓及按揭，署方亦可代為執行並向業主追討費用。A Buildings Department order does not go away by being ignored. It is registered against the title, affecting sale and mortgage, and the Department may carry out the works itself and recover the cost from the owner.

先分清楚收到的是甚麼

屋宇署發出的文件種類不同，應對方式與時限亦不同。常見的包括：

  • 清拆令——針對違例建築工程（僭建物），要求在指定期限內拆除
  • 修葺令——樓宇出現危險或失修狀況，要求進行修葺
  • 勘測令——要求委任專業人士對樓宇進行勘測並提交報告
  • 強制驗樓／驗窗法定通知——要求進行訂明檢驗及修葺
  • 渠務修理通知——要求修理有問題的排水系統

每份文件上都會列明遵辦期限。這個日期是關鍵：即使無法在期限前完成工程，及早展示已委任專業人士、已提交方案的進度，與完全沒有回應，後果差別很大。

為何不能置之不理

三重後果

其一，不遵辦命令屬刑事罪行，可被檢控。其二，命令會在土地註冊處註冊，成為業權上的「污點」，直接影響物業出售、按揭及估價。其三，署方可安排承辦商代為執行工程，其後連同監督費用向業主追討——費用通常高於業主自行安排。

正確的應對步驟

  1. 核對文件內容及期限——確認命令所指的具體位置、要求及遵辦日期。
  2. 委任合適的專業人士——視乎命令性質，可能須委任認可人士、註冊結構工程師或註冊檢驗人員。這一步決定了後續能否順利處理。
  3. 現場勘察及方案——確定實際狀況，區分「必須拆除」與「可循法定途徑規範化」的部分。並非所有僭建物都只有拆除一途，部分可透過入則申請合法化，視乎性質及是否符合現行規例。
  4. 入則及／或施工——按方案提交法定文件，安排註冊承建商施工，由專業人士監督。
  5. 完工及遵辦確認——工程完成後提交所需證明，正式解除命令。

與簡樸房認證的關係

如果您同時持有分間單位，這一點特別重要：未遵辦的法定命令，一般會阻礙簡樸房認證獲批。換言之，命令不是與認證平行的另一件事，而是認證的前置條件。

實務上我們見到的常見情況是：業主先花費完成單位內部改造，臨到申請認證才發現大廈層面有未解除的命令，結果工程完成卻卡在最後一關。合理的做法，是在最初評估階段就一併查核樓宇的命令記錄。

為何一司處理較有效率

解除命令涉及入則簽署（認可人士）、結構評估（註冊結構工程師）及現場檢驗（測量師或註冊檢驗人員）。若這些角色分散在不同公司，業主須自行協調，時間往往消耗在等候與轉交之間。由同一間公司統籌，方案可一次過涵蓋命令處理與其他合規需要，避免重複開工。

本文為一般說明，不構成法律意見。具體個案應按命令內容及專業評估處理。

First, identify what you have received

Different documents carry different requirements and deadlines. Common ones include:

  • Removal order — unauthorised building works to be demolished by a stated date
  • Repair order — dangerous or dilapidated conditions to be repaired
  • Investigation order — appoint a professional to investigate and report
  • Mandatory building/window inspection notice — prescribed inspection and repair
  • Drainage repair notice — defective drainage to be rectified

Each states a compliance date. Even where works cannot finish by then, demonstrating early that a professional has been appointed and a scheme submitted puts an owner in a very different position from silence.

Why ignoring it is the expensive option

Three consequences

First, non-compliance is a criminal offence and may be prosecuted. Second, the order is registered at the Land Registry and becomes an encumbrance on title, affecting sale, mortgage and valuation. Third, the Department may arrange the works itself and recover the cost, plus supervision charges, from the owner — usually more than arranging it yourself.

The right sequence

  1. Check the document and the deadline — the exact location, requirement and compliance date.
  2. Appoint the right professional — depending on the order, an Authorized Person, Registered Structural Engineer or Registered Inspector.
  3. Inspect and form a scheme — separate what must be removed from what may be regularised. Not every unauthorised work has to come down; some can be legitimised through submission, depending on its nature and compliance with current regulations.
  4. Submit and/or build — statutory submissions, registered contractor, professional supervision.
  5. Complete and discharge — file the required certification so the order is formally cleared.

How this connects to BHU certification

If you also hold subdivided units, note this carefully: an outstanding statutory order will generally block Basic Housing Unit certification. The order is not a parallel issue — it is a precondition.

The pattern we see: an owner completes internal works, then discovers at application stage that the building carries an unresolved order, and everything stalls at the final step. Order records should be checked at the initial assessment.

Why one practice is faster

Discharging an order involves submission signature (Authorized Person), structural assessment (Registered Structural Engineer) and site inspection (surveyor or Registered Inspector). Split across firms, the owner ends up coordinating and waiting. Handled in one practice, the scheme can address the order and other compliance needs at once, avoiding repeat works.
